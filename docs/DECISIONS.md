2026-07-23 — Self-reported facts the enrichment record doesn't confirm are weak evidence. Established while judging #30's batch 3: two leads claimed a CRM they didn't demonstrably run — Ignite Visibility (claimed HubSpot; site scraped successfully, HubSpot not detected) and Englewood Construction (claimed a basic CRM; site scrape failed entirely, nothing checkable). A successful scrape that fails to find a claimed tool is closer to contradiction than absence, so it warrants lower confidence than simply-missing data. Rule adopted for human scoring and proposed for the prompt: score claimed-but-unverified tooling low, and never treat a lead's self-report as equivalent to a detected fact. Relates to the fabricated-headcount issue (#36) — the same gap, in the opposite direction: the rubric specifies what to score but never how to weigh evidence quality.
